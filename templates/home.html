Drowsy driving is a serious problem regarding road safety. Most of the road accidents can be avoided or their effect can be minimized by taking proper precautions.

What leads to drowsy driving?

Lack of enough sleep

Get enough sleep! Adults need 7 or 8 hours of sleep a day, while adolescents need 9 or 10 hours. If you have a sleep disorder, make sure to seek treatment. The less sleep you get, the more likely you are to fall asleep while driving.

Do you work long or during irregular hours?

Working on a night shift is likely to increase your risk of drowsy driving. Employees working on extended shifts or more than one job are prone to experience sleepiness.

Medication Side-Effects

Sleepiness is one of the most commonly reported side effects of medications and drugs, and itâ€™s an important one to note and discuss with your doctor. Excessive sleepiness can be caused by the sedating effects of nonprescription and prescription medications

Naturally occurring circadian factors

This is where our body clock naturally regulates our body to sleep at specific times. There is an increase in the brain's drive to sleep during the mid-afternoons.
